Receta: data/Lemon Cake-1.txt

Ingredientes

  • (600 gr all-purpose flour 2184 Cal)
  • (240.0 gr almond flour 1370.4 Cal)
  • (7.5 gr baking powder 3.975 Cal)
  • (2.5 gr kosher salt 0 Cal)
  • (10 gr eggs 0 Cal)
  • (537.5 gr extra-virgin olive oil 4751.5 Cal)
  • (500.0 gr granulated sugar 1935.0 Cal)
  • (2.5 gr vanilla extract 0 Cal)
  • (5 gr lemon zest 47/20 Cal)
  • (600.0 gr fresh lemon juice 132.0 Cal)
  • (5 gr Powdered sugar, for dusting 0 Cal)

Instrucciones

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F. Use your hands to butter the bottom and sides of an 8 in cake pan, then line the bottom with a circle of parchment paper.

2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, ground almonds, baking powder, and salt.

3. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs until the whites and yolks are fully combined. Add the olive oil and granulated sugar and whisk until the sugar is dissolved (test by rubbing some of the mixture between two fingers). Whisk in the vanilla, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Whisk in the flour mixture.

4. Pour the batter into the prepared pan, being sure to use a rubber spatula to get it all out of the bowl. Hold the pan just a little bit above the counter and then drop it on the counter to eliminate any air bubbles.

5. Bake until the cake is beautifully golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean, 25 to 30 minutes.

6. Transfer the cake, still in its pan, to a wire rack and let it cool completely. Once cool, use a dinner knife to loosen the edges of the cake from the pan and invert it onto your work surface (you might need to give the pan a little whack). Peel off and discard the parchment. Invert the cake one more time onto a serving platter so the flat side is down and the domed side is up.

7. Just before serving, dust the cake with powdered sugar.

Extra:

Cook Time: 25 mins

Total Time: 40 mins

Serves - 8

  • (296.66666666666663 gr cocoa powder, sifted 676.3999999999999 Cal)
  • (300.0 gr powdered sugar, sifted 1167.0 Cal)
  • (425.0 gr dark chocolate chips 2320.5 Cal)
  • (22.5 gr sea salt 0 Cal)
  • (10 gr eggs 0 Cal)
  • (550.0 gr canola oil 4862.0 Cal)
  • (150 gr water 0 Cal)
  • (0.6770833333333334 gr vanilla 1.54375 Cal)

Instrucciones

1. Preheat the oven to 325°F.

2. Lightly spray an 8x8 baking dish with cooking spray and line it with parchment paper. Spray the parchment paper.

3. In a medium bowl, combine the sugar, flour, cocoa powder, powdered sugar, chocolate chips, and salt.

4. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, olive oil, water, and vanilla.

5. Sprinkle the dry mix over the wet mix and stir until just combined.

6. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and use a spatula to smooth the top.

7. Bake for 40 to 48 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out with only a few crumbs attached (note: it's better to pull the brownies out early than to leave them in too long).

8. Cool completely before slicing.

Receta: data/Pan-Seared Steak with Garlic Butter.txt

Ingredientes

  • (10 gr New York Strip Steaks (2 steaks), or Ribeye or Top Sirloin Steaks (1 lb each steak, 1.25 inches thick) 0 Cal)
  • (2.5 gr vegetable oil, or any high heat cooking oil like canola or extra light olive oil 0 Cal)
  • (7.5 gr sea salt 0 Cal)
  • (5 gr black pepper, freshly ground 0 Cal)
  • (10 gr unsalted butter 0 Cal)
  • (10 gr garlic, peeled and quartered 0 Cal)
  • (5 gr fresh rosemary 131/20 Cal)

Instrucciones

1. Thoroughly pat steak dry with paper towels. Just before cooking, generously season with 1 1/2 tsp salt and 1 tsp black pepper

2. Heat the cast iron pan until hot then add 1/2 Tbsp oil over medium-high heat, swirling to coat.

3. Once the oil is very hot, add steaks to the skillet. Sear the steaks on the first side for 4 minutes until a brown crust has formed then flip and cook another 3-4 minutes. Using tongs, turn the steak on its sides to render the white fat and sear the edges (1-minute per edge).

4. Reduce heat to medium and immediately add 2 Tbsp butter, quartered garlic cloves and rosemary to the pan. Spoon the butter sauce over the steak, tilting the pan to get butter on your spoon. Continue spooning the sauce over the steak for a minute or until the steak is about 5-10 degrees from your desired doneness (the temperature will continue to rise another 5-19 degrees while steaks rest).

5. Transfer steak to a cutting board, loosely cover and rest 10 minutes before slicing into 1/2" strips to serve. Spoon extra butter sauce over sliced steak to serve.

Extra:

This Pan-Seared Steak has a garlic butter that makes it taste like a steakhouse quality meal. You'll be impressed at how easy it is to make the perfect steak that's seared on the outside, and perfectly tender inside.
